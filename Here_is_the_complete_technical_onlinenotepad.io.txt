The proposed Intelligent WAF is assessed using typical binary classification metrics such as Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1 score, ROC-AUC and False Positive Rate. The proposed Bi-LSTM model is compared with LSTM model, Transformer model, and the ModSecurity CRS v3.
A. Evaluation Metrics
The following metrics are evaluated as a performance test:
Accuracy - percentage of all requests that are correctly classified.
Precision: Proportion of requests that are labeled as attacks which are actually attacks.
Recall: Percentage of actual attacks that are correctly identified.
A harmonic average of precision and recall.F1-score: A harmonic mean of precision and recall.
* ROC-AUC: Evaluates the model's performance in differentiation of normal and attack traffic on thresholds.
False Positive Rate (FPR): Percentage of false alarms from legitimate requests.
A false positive rate is particularly critical to a WAF system as blocking legitimate users can impact usability and business processes.

 C. Discussion of Results:
The proposed Bi-LSTM model gives an accuracy of 96.02%, outperforming the baseline LSTM model, the Transformer model and the ModSecurity CRS v3. The precision of the Bi-LSTM is the best at 99.53%, meaning that it has the fewest false positives of all the models. This is crucial for a WAF since too many false positives can hamper legitimate users.
The Bi-LSTM model achieves an F1-score of 94.92%, which is a good balance between precision and recall. The model demonstrated a good performance with a ROC-AUC of 99.50%, indicating its ability to accurately separate the classes of normal and malicious traffic at all classification levels.
The performance of the LSTM baseline is much lower compared to the proposed Bi-LSTM. This demonstrates that single-direction sequence processing is not enough to effectively capture all the request patterns. Transformer model outperforms LSTM baseline but is still less than Bi-LSTM. It can be attributed to the fact that CSIC 2010 is a moderate size dataset and the Bi-LSTM model is better suited to learning sequential character-level patterns for this dataset.
